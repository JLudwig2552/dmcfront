General

1. How do I get more information about the DMC?

    We have documentation that you can read on our www.opendmc.org website. You can also find a few quick start guides on the DMC Wiki and technical documentation on Read the Docs.

2. How do I become the admin for my company?

    Curently only Tier 1 and Tier 2 DMDII Members have the ability to become administrator of their organizations. In order to become an administrator of an organization, the to be administrator must have an account. Secondly, the organization has to become a member of DMDII. Once it has been confirmed your organization has become a member, the company will be upgraded to a member on the DMC. Someone from DMDII will then reachout to the to be administrator to help them through the process of making them an administrator for their organization.

3. How do I log in?

    Federated authentication is used for logging into the DMC. This means we do not store your login credentails. If your organization has federated with us you will be able to choose your organization from a list of potentail login authendication methods after clicking on the login button on the top right hand corner of the home screen.

4. When creating my account, I did not see my company in the modal. How do I move forward?

    When login in the first time, you did not see your organization in the drop down, you will need to choose 'other' from the list. At this time you are not able to create your own company, but in the future will be prompted to name your company after choosing other.

5. I am a part of organization XYZ, how do I visit my company's profile page?

    To visit your company profile page, click on Marketplace tab on the top of the page, and then on My Storefront.

6. How do I view other company profiles?

    To view other company profiles, click on the Community tab on the top on the page. Next, click on the filter drop-down next to the search-bar near the top of the page, and choose Companies and then click the green search button. You should now see a list of all companies who have created a profile on the DMC. By clicking on the the company name, you will be redirected to the company's profile page.

7. How do I get access to my company's digital DMC assets?

    Once you have created your account and have chosen your affiliated organization, your name will appear in a queue that the organization admin can see. It is the administrator's role to add user who are supposed tp be added and have access to your organization's digital assets such as company profile, member projects and services. If you are not added to the organization in a resonable ammount of time, try reaching out to your organization adminitrator via e-mail, requesting to be added to the to the list of accepted users.

    All services and assets your organization has access to use will then allow you to use them as well.

8. How do I log in with my organization credentials?

    If your organization has federated with the DMC, click on the login button in the top right hand corner of the home page. Then choose your organization from the list. Enter your organization login information to authenticate.

    You should now be logged in.

9. What is a service and what is the process for running one?

    A service is an application that was created in Excel, Matlab, Java, Python or somethng else, but has a wrapper that allows the interaction with the application to be minimal. There is no need to change inything in the code itself. The user is shown a simple inface with inputs and outputs only. These application are special since it does not require the original application to be installed or run on your computer or server. The application runs in the browser.

    You can begin running a service by doing the following:
    1. Click on your name in the top right hand corner of the screen.
      1. Click on My Account.
    3. On the left hand side of the screen, click on SERVERS.
      1. Click on Add Server.
        1. The alias is what you want to name the server.
        2. The default Server URL for the DOME serve is: 52.91.102.101
        3. The Port Number for the Default DOME Server is: 8080.
        4. Click on the green Save button.
    5. Click on Projects in the top menu.
      1. Then choose My Projects from the drop down menu.
    7. Click on Add Project in the center of the page.
      1. Fill in all of the information on the page.
        1. For this time, make sure to make your projects private. Make sure to give your project a name, select a start date for the project from the calendar (or enter it manually, making sure the date format is correct), and provide a project tag if you are interested.
    8. Click next in the bottom right hand corner of the page.
      1. Fill out this page as well. For the time being do not add others to your project.
    9. Click the green create button.
    10. Under services, click view all.
      1. If you recieve an error, the project should still have been created. Return to the My Projects page by following the previous steps. Now, click on the project name.
    11. To the right of the service section, click on VIEW ALL.
    12. Click on REGISTER SERVICES on the right hand side of the screen.
    13. Click on the server field and choose the server alias you just created.
      1. Click on the green NEXT button.
    14. Choose the service you want to run.
    15. Enter the inputs for the service and click the green run button.


Members Portal

1. My organization is a member of DMDII, why don't I see the member portal?

    There could be many reasons why your organization is not listed in the list of options when logging in. If your organization has chosen not to set up a company profile on the DMC, it will not show up as an option.

    Your organization administrator may not have accepted your request yet. You may need to reach out to you organization adminstrator to accept your request.

    The process for getting verified is as follows: Your company administrator will send you a token (a secret key) paste this key in the verification token field.
    Follow these steps to submit your verification token:
    1. Click on your name in the top right hand portion of the page.
      1. In the drop-down menu, click on My Account.
    2. Paste in your verification token in the appropriate field.
    3. Click on the green verify button.

2. How do I become a member of DMDII?

    You can learn more about DMDII, membership, and it's benefits by following this link.

3. What useful information can I find in the member portal?

    The member portal contains frequently updated information about recent member news, DMDII events news, a list of all current DMDII members and what projects they are working on. In addition to a list of all current projects, financial and updated information regarding projects. DMDII will also share quick links such as documents and links to they wish to share with all members.


Contributing

1. How do I contribute to the DMC?

    You can look at our latest updated code on our Bitbucket repository. If you are interested in contributing code to the DMC, please get in contact with us via e-mail or Slack. You can find all of our modes of contact on our Contact-Us page. You can also help us by building application that can live in the DMC by building DOME models.

    You can also look at our Contact Us page for more information.


Running your own Instance of the DMC in your Organization

1. What does hosting the DMC internally in my organization do for me?

    Running an instance of the DMC allows you to host the platform behiond your firewall. This means your organization is responcible for keeping your information protected, but does not need to worry about uploading proprietary information to the internet. The DMC is already a safe place to upload your data, but if you would feel safer with your information being hosted locally, this will be an option.

2. What is the process for this?

    We will offer documentation on the process of setting up a DMC instance to run within your organization in the futute.

3. Can we get assistance in setting this up?

    We are still heavily working on development but we will eventually be offering this as a service.

4. I'm not a programer. Can I still contribute or get involved?

    Yes, of course! We are looking for help from anyone who is willing to donate their time. Visit our wiki to find out about other ways of contributing.
